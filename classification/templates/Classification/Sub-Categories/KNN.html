About k-Nearest Neighbours Classification

A k-nearest-neighbor algorithm, often abbreviated k-NN, is an approach to data classification that estimates how likely a data point is to be a member of one group or the other depending on what group the data points nearest to it are in.

Requirements

If training data is large and non linearly separable, then we use KNN.

Applications

  • Credit ratings
    • Should the bank give a loan to an individual? Would an individual default on his or her loan? Is that person closer in characteristics to people who defaulted or did not default on their loans?
